# Platform Differences

## Why This Matters

Each dating app has different norms, mechanics, and user expectations. What works on Hinge won't necessarily work on Tinder, and Bumble's women-first dynamic changes the entire opening strategy. Understanding the platform helps you calibrate tone, length, and approach.

---

## Hinge

### Mechanics
- Your "opener" is a comment on one of her prompts or photos (not a separate message)
- Character limit: ~200 characters for comments
- She sees your full profile alongside your comment before deciding to match
- If she "matches" (accepts your like + comment), the conversation begins
- Roses signal especially high interest - use sparingly, pair with your best comment

### Culture
- Relationship-oriented ("designed to be deleted")
- Text-heavy - prompts invite substantial responses
- Users expect more thought and effort than other platforms
- Thoughtful > clever > funny > generic (all are fine, but thoughtfulness is the baseline expectation)

### Messaging Norms
- **First message:** Already sent as your comment. If she matches, she might respond to your comment or send something new.
- **Tone:** Slightly more intentional than Tinder. Casual but shows you read her profile.
- **Length:** 1-3 sentences typical. Don't write a paragraph, but one word is too little.
- **Openers that reference prompts** outperform openers that reference photos (Hinge Labs data). Her prompts are things she chose to say - they're better personalization material.

### Strategy Tips
- Comment on prompts > photos (she chose her words; photos are more passive)
- Your profile does half the work - if it's strong, your comment doesn't need to be perfect
- "Most Compatible" suggestions are worth paying attention to (algorithm is reasonably good)
- Don't mass-like without comments - bare likes get ignored
- Roses: use on someone you're genuinely very interested in, paired with your most thoughtful comment

## Bumble

### Mechanics
- In heterosexual matches, **she messages first** (24-hour window or match expires)
- After she opens, you have 24 hours to reply (or extend)
- No structured prompt-comment system - it's a full chat from the start
- Women can extend one match per day (gives you 24 more hours)

### Culture
- Positions itself as empowering for women
- Slightly more relationship-oriented than Tinder
- Users tend to be somewhat more intentional
- Her opener quality varies wildly (many women aren't used to opening)

### The Flip: Your Reply IS Your Opener

Since she messages first, your first reply is effectively your opener. Most women's first messages are low-effort:
- "Hey!"
- "Your dog is so cute!"
- "I liked your answer about [prompt]!"
- A question from your profile

**Strategy:** Acknowledge her message briefly (don't ignore it), then take conversational initiative. Pivot to something from HER profile.

**Example:**
> Her: "Hey! I loved your hiking photo 🏔️"
> Bad reply: "Thanks! How are you?"
> Good reply: "Thanks! That was Mount [X] last fall - genuinely terrifying and gorgeous. I see you're into [thing from her profile] - what's the story there?"

### Messaging Norms
- **Reply promptly** (24-hour window creates urgency)
- **Take the lead** - don't put the conversation labor back on her
- **Match her energy** but escalate slightly (she opened, you drive)
- **Tone:** Similar to Hinge but slightly less formal. Warm, curious, engaging.

### Strategy Tips
- Your profile quality matters more here because she can't comment on specifics - she has to decide to match and then generate an opener from scratch
- Don't wait until the last minute to reply (it looks disinterested)
- If her opener is truly bare-bones ("hey"), don't judge - many women haven't built the opener muscle. Treat it as a green light and carry the first exchange

## Tinder

### Mechanics
- Swipe-based matching (mutual swipes = match)
- No structured prompts - just bio (500 char limit) and photos
- Either person can message first after matching
- Super Likes signal higher interest (use sparingly)
- Most casual and highest-volume platform

### Culture
- Originally hookup-focused, now mixed intentions
- Most casual of the three platforms
- Highest volume = most noise to cut through
- Less profile content means less to work with for personalization
- Shorter attention spans - differentiation matters most here

### Messaging Norms
- **Shorter messages** are the norm (platform culture is more casual)
- **Faster escalation** - window to ask for a date is shorter because attention is more fleeting
- **Humor-forward** - playfulness and wit stand out more because the bar is lower
- **Photo-based openers** are more common (less text to reference)
- **Bio-based** if she has a good bio - but many are empty or minimal

### When Her Bio Is Empty
This is common on Tinder. Your options:
- Reference a specific photo detail (activity, location, outfit, something in the background)
- Make an observation + playful question about something visible
- Use a creative hypothetical or would-you-rather as an opener (last resort - less personalized)
- If there's truly nothing to work with: "I'm going to need you to meet me halfway here - your profile gives me absolutely nothing and I refuse to just say hey. Tell me one interesting thing about yourself."

### Strategy Tips
- Stand out from "hey" and "you're hot" (the two most common messages women receive)
- Keep it punchy - 1-2 sentences max for openers
- Escalate to date ask faster than other platforms (5-10 messages is often the sweet spot)
- Don't over-invest in long conversations - Tinder attention spans are shorter
- If she matched, she's at least mildly interested - the bar to clear is lower than you think

## Universal Principles (All Platforms)

These transcend platform norms:

1. **Personalization always wins** - reference something specific to HER, regardless of platform
2. **Specificity always wins** - concrete details over vague categories
3. **Humor always helps** - calibrate the style to the platform (wittier on Hinge, punchier on Tinder)
4. **Value exchange always matters** - every message should offer something
5. **The Banter Wave Theory applies everywhere** - platforms just change the pacing
6. **Signal reading is universal** - the same investment indicators work across all platforms
7. **Voice consistency matters** - sound like yourself whether you're on Hinge or Tinder
8. **Direct date asks beat vague ones** - on every platform

## Platform Selection Guidance

If the user asks which platform to prioritize:

| Looking for... | Best platform |
|---|---|
| Serious relationship | Hinge (best signal-to-noise for relationship-oriented matches) |
| Relationship, women-first dynamic | Bumble (less opener pressure, intentional users) |
| Casual dating, high volume | Tinder (biggest user base, fastest pace) |
| All of the above | Hinge primary, Bumble secondary, Tinder if bandwidth allows |

**General advice:** Don't spread yourself too thin. 2-3 quality conversations on one platform beat 10 half-dead conversations across four platforms. Depth > breadth.
