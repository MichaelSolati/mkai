# Research Findings

The evidence base for message-crafter. Every recommendation maps back to one of three evidence tiers:

| Tier | Meaning | How to use |
|------|---------|------------|
| **Research** | Peer-reviewed study with methodology you can evaluate | Treat as reliable; cite to users when explaining "why" |
| **Platform Data** | Published by dating apps (OkCupid blog, Hinge reports, internal data releases) | Treat as directionally reliable; platforms have incentives to share selectively |
| **Conventional Wisdom** | Consistent anecdotal evidence from dating coaches, power users, and practitioners | Useful heuristic; acknowledge uncertainty |

---

## 1. Specificity and Response Rates

**Finding:** Messages referencing specific profile content get significantly higher reply rates than generic openers.

| Source | Evidence Tier | Key Data |
|--------|--------------|----------|
| Rudder, C. (2014). *Dataclysm*. OkCupid internal data. | Platform Data | Generic messages ("hey", "what's up") get reply rates under 30%. Messages referencing specific profile content get 40-60% higher reply rates. |
| Hinge Labs (2018-2022). Multiple reports on comment engagement. | Platform Data | Comments on prompts outperform comments on photos. Specific, observational comments outperform generic compliments. |

**Practical implication:** Every opener must reference something specific to her profile. "Hey" is the cheapest possible signal of interest.

---

## 2. Signaling Theory Applied to Messaging

**Finding:** High-effort, personalized messages are "costly signals" of genuine interest. Low-effort messages are "cheap signals" that get filtered out.

| Source | Evidence Tier | Key Data |
|--------|--------------|----------|
| Donath, J. (2007). "Signals in Social Supernets." *Journal of Computer-Mediated Communication*. | Research | Costly signals (time-intensive, hard to fake, specific) are more credible indicators of interest than cheap signals (copy-paste, generic, mass-sendable). |
| Toma, C. & Hancock, J. (2012). "What Lies Beneath: The Linguistic Traces of Deception in Online Dating Profiles." *Journal of Communication*. | Research | Specific language signals honesty; vague language signals evasion or deception. Recipients implicitly parse this. |

**Practical implication:** A personalized message that took 30 seconds to write signals more genuine interest than "hey beautiful" sent to 50 people. The effort IS the message.

---

## 3. Self-Disclosure and Attraction

**Finding:** Graduated, reciprocal self-disclosure builds intimacy and attraction. Sharing too much too fast or too little creates imbalance.

| Source | Evidence Tier | Key Data |
|--------|--------------|----------|
| Altman, I. & Taylor, D. (1973). *Social Penetration Theory*. | Research | Relationships deepen through incremental layers of self-disclosure - breadth first, then depth. |
| Sprecher, S., Treger, S., & Wondra, J. (2013). "Effects of self-disclosure role on liking, closeness, and other impressions." *Journal of Social and Personal Relationships*. | Research | Reciprocal disclosure (both parties sharing at similar depth) produces more liking than one-sided disclosure. The listener AND the discloser both feel closer. |
| Collins, N. & Miller, L. (1994). "Self-disclosure and liking: A meta-analytic review." *Psychological Bulletin*. | Research | People who disclose are liked more, and people disclose more to those they like - a positive feedback loop. |

**Practical implication:** Don't just ask questions - share something too. Match her disclosure depth, then edge slightly deeper. The "interviewer trap" (all questions, no sharing) kills reciprocity.

---

## 4. Humor in Messaging

**Finding:** Humor production signals intelligence, creativity, and social savvy. It significantly increases response rates and attraction.

| Source | Evidence Tier | Key Data |
|--------|--------------|----------|
| McGee, E. & Shevlin, M. (2009). "Effect of humor on interpersonal attraction and mate selection." *Journal of Psychology*. | Research | Humor production (being funny) is a stronger predictor of attraction than humor appreciation (finding things funny). |
| Bressler, E. & Balshine, S. (2006). "The influence of humor on desirability." *Evolution and Human Behavior*. | Research | Women are more attracted to men who produce humor than to men who appreciate humor. |
| OkCupid Blog (2011). "Exactly What To Say In A First Message." | Platform Data | Humorous openers get ~12% higher reply rates than earnest/straightforward ones. |
| Hall, J. (2015). "Sexual selection and humor in courtship." *Evolutionary Psychology*. | Research | Shared laughter (not just one-sided jokes) is the strongest humor-related predictor of romantic interest. |

**Practical implication:** Humor is a tool, not a requirement. Use it to demonstrate personality, create shared moments, and lower tension. Forced humor backfires. Observational, playful, low-stakes humor works best.

## 5. Message Length and Outcomes

**Finding:** Message length has a Goldilocks zone - too short signals low effort, too long signals over-investment.

| Source | Evidence Tier | Key Data |
|--------|--------------|----------|
| Hinge Labs (2019). Engagement data on message length. | Platform Data | Opening comments between 40-90 characters outperform very short (<20) or very long (>150) messages on Hinge. |
| OkCupid Blog (2011). First message analysis. | Platform Data | Messages between 40-100 words get the best reply rates. Very short (1-10 words) and very long (200+) both underperform. |

**Practical implication:** Openers: punchy, 1-2 sentences. Mid-conversation: match her length. Never write a paragraph when she's sending sentences. Never send one word when she's investing paragraphs.

## 6. Question-Asking and Liking

**Finding:** People who ask more questions - especially follow-up questions - are liked more.

| Source | Evidence Tier | Key Data |
|--------|--------------|----------|
| Huang, K., Yeomans, M., Brooks, A., Minson, J., & Gino, F. (2017). "It Doesn't Hurt to Ask: Question-Asking Increases Liking." *Journal of Personality and Social Psychology*. | Research | Follow-up questions (referencing what someone just said) are particularly effective because they signal active listening. Switch-topic questions are less effective. |

**Practical implication:** Follow-up questions > new-topic questions. "You hiked in Peru - what was the wildest part?" beats "So what do you do for work?" Callback references to earlier topics demonstrate genuine attention.

## 7. Flirting Styles and Effectiveness

**Finding:** There are distinct flirting styles; sincere + playful is most effective in text-based communication.

| Source | Evidence Tier | Key Data |
|--------|--------------|----------|
| Hall, J., Carter, S., Cody, M., & Albright, J. (2010). "Individual Differences in the Communication of Romantic Interest." *Communication Quarterly*. | Research | Five flirting styles: playful, sincere, physical, traditional, polite. Sincere flirting (showing genuine interest) combined with playful flirting (light humor, banter) predicts the most success in initial messaging. |

**Practical implication:** The best messages combine genuine interest (sincerity) with light humor (playfulness). Pure sincerity without levity reads as intense. Pure playfulness without substance reads as unserious.

---

## 8. Compliment Effectiveness

**Finding:** Personality/behavior compliments outperform appearance compliments in initial messaging.

| Source | Evidence Tier | Key Data |
|--------|--------------|----------|
| Buss, D. & Schmitt, D. (1993). "Sexual Strategies Theory." *Psychological Review*. Evolved framework applied to modern contexts. | Research | Physical compliments from strangers activate defensive responses rather than attraction - they signal interest in appearance over personality. |
| OkCupid Blog (2009). "Your Looks And Your Inbox." | Platform Data | Messages complimenting appearance ("you're beautiful", "you're hot") get lower reply rates than messages engaging with personality or interests. |

**Practical implication:** Compliment what she chose (prompt answers, humor, taste, opinions) not what she was given (appearance). "Your take on [X] is hilarious" beats "you're gorgeous."

---

## 9. First-Date Conversion

**Finding:** Specific, authentic pre-date messaging predicts better first-date outcomes and higher likelihood of a second date.

| Source | Evidence Tier | Key Data |
|--------|--------------|----------|
| Sharabi, L. & Caughlin, J. (2017). "What predicts first date success? A longitudinal study of modality switching in online dating." *Personal Relationships*. | Research | Deeper self-disclosure and more specific communication before meeting predicted better first-date experiences and more second dates. Generic small talk did not predict success. |

**Practical implication:** The quality of pre-date messaging directly influences date quality. This isn't just about getting the date - it's about having a good one. Invest in real conversation before meeting.

---

## 10. Pacing and Reciprocity

**Finding:** Communication norms create implicit contracts. Violating expected pacing (too fast, too much) triggers discomfort.

| Source | Evidence Tier | Key Data |
|--------|--------------|----------|
| Gouldner, A. (1960). "The Norm of Reciprocity." *American Sociological Review*. | Research | Reciprocity is a universal social norm - people feel obligated to match the level of investment they receive. Over-investing creates uncomfortable obligation rather than attraction. |
| Walther, J. (1996). "Computer-Mediated Communication: Impersonal, Interpersonal, and Hyperpersonal Interaction." *Communication Research*. | Research | The "Hyperpersonal Model": text-based communication lacks non-verbal cues, so recipients fill gaps with assumptions. Pacing and effort become proxy signals for interest and social calibration. |

**Practical implication:** Mirror her response cadence. Don't send three messages when she's sent one. Don't reply in 5 seconds when she takes hours. Match investment levels - then edge slightly above.

## 11. Platform-Specific Data

| Source | Evidence Tier | Key Data |
|--------|--------------|----------|
| Tyson, G. et al. (2016). "A First Look at User Activity on Tinder." *IEEE/ACM ASONAM*. | Research | Men swipe right ~62% on Tinder vs. women ~6%. This asymmetry means women are overwhelmed with matches and are more selective about which conversations they invest in. |
| Hinge (2022). "The Dating Apocalypse Is Not Happening." Company report. | Platform Data | Top 10% of men receive 58% of all likes. Differentiation is the only viable strategy - volume approaches fail. |
| Bumble (2021-2023). Various press releases on messaging behavior. | Platform Data | Women-first messaging reduces match volume but increases conversation quality. Men's replies to women's openers have higher engagement when they take conversational initiative. |

**Practical implication:** She has options. Your message competes with dozens of others. Differentiation through specificity, personality, and genuine engagement is the only strategy that works at scale.

---

## Key Takeaways

1. **Specificity is the single most predictive factor** in whether a message gets a reply (Rudder, 2014; Donath, 2007; Hinge data)
2. **Reciprocal self-disclosure** builds connection faster than question-asking alone (Sprecher et al., 2013)
3. **Humor combined with sincerity** is the optimal messaging style (Hall et al., 2010; McGee & Shevlin, 2009)
4. **Follow-up questions** outperform new-topic questions (Huang et al., 2017)
5. **Message quality before meeting** directly predicts date quality (Sharabi & Caughlin, 2017)
6. **Pacing and investment matching** signal social calibration (Walther, 1996; Gouldner, 1960)
7. **Effort is visible** - costly signals (personalization, specificity) are credible; cheap signals (generic, mass-sendable) are not (Donath, 2007)
