# Opening Messages

## The 3-Element Framework

Every great opener contains three elements:

1. **Personalization** - Reference something specific from her profile (prompt answer, photo detail, bio element)
2. **Open-ended question** - Invite elaboration, never a yes/no dead end
3. **Spark of personality** - Humor, wit, or shared enthusiasm that gives her a reason to want to know you

You don't need all three every time - sometimes two is enough. But you always need personalization. A generic opener is an instant delete.

## The Bad → Better → Best Progression

### Scenario 1: Her prompt says she loves hiking and has a photo from Peru with a llama

- **Bad:** "Hey."
- **Better:** "Cool hiking pic! Where was that?"
- **Best:** "That photo with the llama - I genuinely can't tell who's more majestic. What was the wildest part of that trip?"

*Why Best works:* Specific detail (llama), humor (who's more majestic), open-ended question (wildest part), personality (playful observation).

### Scenario 2: Her prompt answer is "The best way to ask me out is: plan something fun and just tell me when to show up"

- **Bad:** "Hey, so when are you free?"
- **Better:** "I like that energy. What's your idea of fun?"
- **Best:** "A woman who wants to be surprised? Dangerous. I'm already mentally planning something involving either a night market or a terrible karaoke bar. Depends - are you more of a 'eat adventurous food' or 'sing badly in public' person?"

*Why Best works:* Shows he actually read the prompt, demonstrates initiative (already planning), gives her an easy conversational choice, humor (terrible karaoke), paints a scene.

### Scenario 3: Her bio mentions she's a nurse who loves bad horror movies

- **Bad:** "Nurses are the real heroes 🙌"
- **Better:** "What's the worst horror movie you've ever genuinely enjoyed?"
- **Best:** "Okay but does being a nurse make horror movies more or less scary? Like do you watch someone get attacked and think 'that wound is survivable, they're being dramatic'?"

*Why Best works:* Connects two things from her profile in an unexpected way, shows creative thinking, invites her professional perspective, humor without trying too hard.

### Scenario 4: Her photo shows her at a pottery class holding a lopsided mug

- **Bad:** "Nice mug!"
- **Better:** "That mug looks like it has character. Did you make it?"
- **Best:** "That mug has a confidence I aspire to. It knows it's lopsided and it doesn't care. Is pottery your thing or was this a one-time adventure?"

*Why Best works:* Personifies the mug (unexpected, playful), self-deprecating confidence, open-ended question that lets her go either direction (regular hobby vs one-time).

### Scenario 5: Her prompt says "I'm weirdly competitive about: board games"

- **Bad:** "Same!"
- **Better:** "What's your game? I'm a Settlers person myself."
- **Best:** "Weirdly competitive about board games is just regular competitive but with more acceptable targets. What's the game where you become a person your friends don't recognize?"

*Why Best works:* Reframes her statement in a funny way (validates it), specific visual (person your friends don't recognize), open-ended, shows he gets it.

### Scenario 6: She's on Bumble and opens with "Hey! I liked your photo at the concert 🎵"

- **Bad:** "Thanks! How are you?"
- **Better:** "Thanks! That was [band name] - one of the best shows I've been to. Are you into live music?"
- **Best:** "That was [band name] at [venue] - genuinely one of the best crowds I've ever been in. Please tell me you have strong opinions about live music because I have a lot of them and I need someone to argue with."

*Why Best works:* Takes the lead (he's driving the conversation even though she opened), specific detail, playful invitation to disagree, shows passion without being preachy.

## Platform-Specific Opener Norms

### Hinge
- Your opener IS a comment on a specific prompt or photo
- Character limit: ~200 characters
- She sees your profile alongside your comment - the two work together
- Best practice: comment on prompts > photos (Hinge data shows prompt-based comments get higher response rates)
- Roses signal high interest - pair with your best, most personalized comment

### Bumble
- She messages first (heterosexual matches)
- Her openers are often low-effort ("hey!", "your dog is so cute!", question from your prompt)
- Your reply is effectively your opener - take conversational initiative
- Strategy: acknowledge her message briefly, then pivot to something from HER profile
- 24-hour expiry creates urgency - respond same day

### Tinder
- No structured prompts - less profile content to work with
- Photo-first platform - openers often reference specific photos
- Shorter messages are the norm (more casual platform)
- Differentiation matters even more here because volume is higher
- Bio is your main text source - if her bio is empty, work from photos

## Common Opener Mistakes

### 1. The Non-Starter
"Hey" / "Hi" / "What's up?" / "How's your day?"

**Why it fails:** Zero effort signal. She gets 20 of these daily. It puts all the conversational labor on her. Signaling theory (Donath, 2007): this is the cheapest possible signal of interest.

### 2. The Generic Compliment
"You're beautiful" / "Love your smile" / "You're stunning"

**Why it fails:** She didn't choose her face - compliment what she chose (her humor, opinions, taste). Appearance compliments from strangers activate defensive responses rather than attraction (Buss & Schmitt, 1993; OkCupid data).

### 3. The Interview Question
"What do you do for work?" / "Where are you from?"

**Why it fails:** No personality, no hooks, no spark. Feels like a job interview. These are fine for message 4-5, not message 1.

### 4. The Overshare
"I've been on this app for months and you're the first person who actually seems interesting..."

**Why it fails:** Signals desperation and over-investment before she's done anything to earn it. Creates uncomfortable pressure.

### 5. The Copy-Paste
Anything that could be sent to anyone without modification.

**Why it fails:** She can tell. It signals she's one of many, not specifically interesting to you. Costly signals theory: zero cost = zero credibility.

### 6. The Try-Hard
A paragraph-long opus analyzing every detail of her profile.

**Why it fails:** Over-investment before she's shown any interest. Comes across as intense rather than charming. Messages between 40-90 characters outperform longer ones (Hinge data).

### 7. The Neg
"You'd be way hotter if..." / "Not usually my type but..."

**Why it fails:** Manipulation disguised as honesty. Creates negative association with you immediately. Research shows compliments > negs in every measurable outcome.

## Principles for Writing Openers

1. **Reference something only she could have** - Not "nice photo" but "the llama in your Peru photo." This proves you looked at HER profile.

2. **Ask something she'd enjoy answering** - Not a chore question ("where do you work?") but something that lets her be interesting ("what's the game where you become a person your friends don't recognize?").

3. **Show your personality through HOW you ask** - The question itself reveals whether you're funny, warm, curious, creative. "Do you like hiking?" vs "What's the view that made you forget you were exhausted and out of water?"

4. **Keep it short** - 1-2 sentences. Punchy. Leave room for her to respond without feeling overwhelmed.

5. **Make it easy to respond to** - If she'd have to think for 5 minutes to answer, it's too complex. The best openers invite a quick, fun response.

6. **Don't front-load compliments** - Lead with curiosity or humor, not admiration. Compliments work better after she's invested in the conversation.

7. **One topic, not three** - Pick the most interesting thing in her profile and focus on it. Don't try to address everything at once.

## Quick-Reference: The Observation → Question Formula

The simplest reliable opener formula:

**[Specific observation about her profile] + [Open-ended question that flows naturally from it]**

Examples:
- "The pottery mug has incredible confidence. Is that your hobby or a one-off?"
- "Ghost peppers?? Are you actually okay? What's the spiciest thing you've genuinely enjoyed?"
- "A Settlers person. Bold. What's your opening strategy - sheep port or ore obsession?"
- "That hiking photo looks like it has a story. What's the short version?"
- "Competitive about board games is just competitive with plausible deniability. What game brings out your worst self?"
